Tenney Harmonic Distance

Two notes form a ratio n∶d (in lowest terms). The Tenney harmonic distance measures how "complex" that ratio is:

HD(n∶d) = log₂(n × d)

Simple ratios like 2∶1 score low (log₂(2) ≈ 1.0) — they share many overtones and sound consonant. Complex ratios like 45∶32 score high (log₂(1440) ≈ 10.5) — few shared overtones, more dissonant.

The bar shows harmonic distance mapped to a 0–10 scale, inverted so longer = more consonant. Unison scores 0; the tritone sits near the top of the scale.

After James Tenney, A History of Consonance and Dissonance (1988).
